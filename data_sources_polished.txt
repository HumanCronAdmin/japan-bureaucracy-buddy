Japan Bureaucracy Buddy - Data Source
=======================================
All data must come from these official/verified sources. No dummy data.

=== Address Registration (Notification of Moving In) === Immigration Bureau:.
- Immigration Bureau: https://www.isa.go.jp/en/
- Website of each municipality (e.g. Shinjuku-ku: https://www.city.shinjuku.lg.jp/foreign/english/).
- Required within 14 days after moving in (Basic Resident Act).

=== My number
- Digital Agency: https://www.digital.go.jp/policies/mynumber/
- My number card portal: https://www.kojinbango-card.go.jp/en/
- Mailed 1-2 months after application

=== Bank account

[MUFG (Mitsubishi UFJ)].
- Official English website: https://www.bk.mufg.jp/global/
- Acceptance of foreigners: YES (6-month rule applies for opening a full account)
- 6-month rule: YES - non-residents get restricted accounts
- English support: limited (some branches and ATMs are English-speaking)
- Required DocumentsPassport, Resident Card, Notification of My Number, personal seal and signature

[Sumitomo Mitsui Banking Corporation
- Official English: https://www.smbc.co.jp/global/
- Foreigners accepted (with 6-month rule)
- 6-month rule: YES
- English support available: Limited (Phone calls are available in English: 0120-327-236)
- Required documentsPassport, resident card, seal or signature

[Mizuho]
- Official English: https://www.mizuhogroup.com/bank
- Foreigners accepted (6-month rule applies)
- 6-month rule: YES
- English support limited
- Required DocumentsPassport, Residence Card

[Japan Post Bank (Yucho)
- Official English: https://www.jp-bank.japanpost.jp/en/
- Acceptable for foreign nationals (Visa remaining period of 3 months or more is acceptable)
- 6 month rule: Exception: Can be opened with a visa of 3 months or more
- English Support: Required Documents: Minimum (English form available)
- Required documents: Residence card, passport
- Remarks: Most friendly to new arrivals.

[Sony Bank]
- Official: https://moneykit.net/en/
- Foreigners accepted: Yes (Residence card + Japanese address required)
- 6-month rule: Applies to first-time applicants only
- English support FULL (application and website are in English)
- Required documents Residence card, My number
- Remarks: Popular among expatriates for its English interface and low FX commissions

[Rakuten Bank]
- Official: https://www.rakuten-bank.co.jp/
- Foreigners accepted: Yes (online only, Japanese address required)
- 6 month rule applies
- English support partially available (translated pages available)
- Required documents: Residence card, My number
- Remarks: Fully online account opening, affiliated with Rakuten Card

=== Health insurance
- Ministry of Health, Labor and Welfare: https://www.mhlw.go.jp/english/
- NHI Summary: https://www.mhlw.go.jp/english/policy/health-medical/health-insurance/
- Enroll at the ward office within 14 days of arrival.
- Premiums are determined based on the previous year's income (cheaper the first year).

=== National Pension
- Japan Pension Service: https://www.nenkin.go.jp/international/
- Enrollment at ward/city hall
- Monthly premium: Approx. 16,980 yen (FY2025)
- Special payment system for students is available.
- Lump-sum withdrawal payment upon leaving Japan.

=== Insurance rules for FY2027
- Source Source: GaijinPot's report on immigration policy changes
- From 2027: Health insurance premium arrears will be checked at the time of visa renewal.
- Non-payment of premiums may result in denial of visa renewal
- Applies to all visa categories

=== Differences by visa category (build must implement these filters)

[Permanent Residence]
- Bank: no 6 month limit
- Insurance Drug or Employment Insurance
- Mandatory pension coverage (no exemptions)
- Special: no visa renewal required if insured

[Engineer/Humanities/International Services (Engineer Country)
- Banking: 6-month rule applies if just arrived
- Insurance: usually paid by employer (social insurance)
- Pension Employer Pension
- Special: employer often handles much of the paperwork.

Student
- Bank 6-month rule applies.
- Insurance NHI (required)
- Pension Student Waiver can be applied for.
- Special Work up to 28 hours per week (permission required)

[Specified Skilled Worker (Specified Skills)
- Bank 6-month rule applies
- InsuranceEmployment insurance (company insurance)
- Pension Employer Pension
- Special: Employer/agency often assists with procedures.

[Spouse of Japanese national]
- Bank If you have just arrived in Japan, the 6-month rule may apply
- Insurance National Health Insurance or spouse's employment insurance
- Pension Category 3 if spouse is a company employee (not required to pay)
- Can work without special restrictions

=== 6-month rule under the Foreign Exchange and Foreign Trade Act
- Foreign Exchange and Foreign Trade Law (Foreign Exchange Law)
- Residence period of less than 6 months = "Non-resident" status
- Non-resident account online banking not available, debit card not available, ATM usage time limited
- Upgradeable to full resident account after 6 months
- Source: Bank of Japan regulations, Wise Blog, Japan Dev

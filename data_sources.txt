Japan Bureaucracy Buddy — Data Sources
=======================================
All data must come from these official/verified sources. No dummy data.

=== Address Registration (Tennyuu Todoke) ===
- Immigration Services Agency: https://www.isa.go.jp/en/
- Each city/ward office website (e.g., Shinjuku: https://www.city.shinjuku.lg.jp/foreign/english/)
- Required within 14 days of moving in (Resident Basic Act)

=== My Number ===
- Digital Agency: https://www.digital.go.jp/policies/mynumber/
- My Number Card portal: https://www.kojinbango-card.go.jp/en/
- Card arrives by mail 1-2 months after application

=== Bank Accounts ===

[MUFG (Mitsubishi UFJ)]
- Official English: https://www.bk.mufg.jp/global/
- Foreigner acceptance: YES (6-month rule applies for full account)
- 6-month rule: YES — non-residents get restricted account
- English support: Limited (some branches, ATM has English)
- Required docs: Passport, Residence Card, My Number notification, seal/signature

[SMBC (Sumitomo Mitsui)]
- Official English: https://www.smbc.co.jp/global/
- Foreigner acceptance: YES (6-month rule applies)
- 6-month rule: YES
- English support: Limited (phone has English line: 0120-327-236)
- Required docs: Passport, Residence Card, inkan or signature

[Mizuho]
- Official English: https://www.mizuhogroup.com/bank
- Foreigner acceptance: YES (6-month rule applies)
- 6-month rule: YES
- English support: Limited
- Required docs: Passport, Residence Card

[Japan Post Bank (Yucho)]
- Official English: https://www.jp-bank.japanpost.jp/en/
- Foreigner acceptance: YES (3+ months of remaining visa period OK)
- 6-month rule: EXCEPTION — can open with 3+ months visa
- English support: Minimal (forms available in English)
- Required docs: Residence Card, Passport
- Note: Most foreigner-friendly for new arrivals

[Sony Bank]
- Official: https://moneykit.net/en/
- Foreigner acceptance: YES (need residence card + Japanese address)
- 6-month rule: Applies for initial application
- English support: FULL (app and website in English)
- Required docs: Residence Card, My Number
- Note: Popular among expats for English interface and low FX fees

[Rakuten Bank]
- Official: https://www.rakuten-bank.co.jp/
- Foreigner acceptance: YES (online only, need Japanese address)
- 6-month rule: Applies
- English support: Partial (some pages translated)
- Required docs: Residence Card, My Number
- Note: Fully online account opening; paired with Rakuten Card

=== Health Insurance ===
- MHLW: https://www.mhlw.go.jp/english/
- NHI overview: https://www.mhlw.go.jp/english/policy/health-medical/health-insurance/
- Enrollment at ward/city office within 14 days of arrival
- Premiums based on previous year income (first year is low)

=== National Pension ===
- Japan Pension Service: https://www.nenkin.go.jp/international/
- Enrollment at ward/city office
- Monthly premium: approximately 16,980 yen (FY2025)
- Students can apply for exemption (学生納付特例)
- Lump-sum withdrawal available when leaving Japan

=== 2027 Insurance Rule ===
- Source: GaijinPot reporting on Immigration Bureau policy change
- From 2027: Health insurance delinquency will be checked at visa renewal
- Unpaid premiums may result in visa renewal denial
- This affects all visa categories

=== Visa-Type Differences (build must implement these filters) ===

[Permanent Resident]
- Bank: No 6-month restriction
- Insurance: NHI or employer insurance
- Pension: Mandatory (no exemption)
- Special: No visa renewal concern for insurance

[Engineer/Specialist in Humanities/International Services (技人国)]
- Bank: 6-month rule applies if just arrived
- Insurance: Usually employer-provided (shakai hoken)
- Pension: Employer pension (kosei nenkin)
- Special: Employer often handles much of the paperwork

[Student]
- Bank: 6-month rule applies; JP Post Bank recommended
- Insurance: NHI (mandatory)
- Pension: Can apply for student exemption
- Special: Work limited to 28 hrs/week (need permission)

[Specified Skilled Worker (特定技能)]
- Bank: 6-month rule applies
- Insurance: Employer insurance (shakai hoken)
- Pension: Employer pension
- Special: Employer/agency often assists with procedures

[Spouse of Japanese National]
- Bank: 6-month rule may apply if just arrived
- Insurance: NHI or spouse's employer insurance
- Pension: Category 3 if spouse is company employee (no payment needed)
- Special: Can work without restriction

=== FEFTA 6-Month Rule ===
- Foreign Exchange and Foreign Trade Act (外為法)
- Residents in Japan less than 6 months = "non-resident" status
- Non-resident accounts: No online banking, no debit card, limited ATM hours
- After 6 months: Can upgrade to full resident account
- Source: Bank of Japan regulations, Wise blog, Japan Dev

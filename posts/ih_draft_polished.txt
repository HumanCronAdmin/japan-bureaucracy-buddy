# Creating a tool for foreigners in Japan: a one-person trip.
## The problem I see
Having lived in Japan for some time now, I have seen many foreigners struggle with the bureaucracy after arriving in Japan. From registering with government offices to opening a bank account, it can be overwhelming. I wanted to create something that would make this process easier.

## What I created
I created a series of free tools, including interactive checklists, to help foreigners navigate the Japanese bureaucracy. The checklists are filtered by visa type so users know exactly what they need to do. I also created 12 other tools, ranging from a Japanese address generator to a guide to finding an apartment.

## What worked/what didn't work
What worked well was that we focused on specific pain points and created a simple, easy-to-use solution. I was also lucky enough to post on subreddits like r/japanlife and r/movingtojapan, which brought some initial traffic to my site. What didn't work out was trying to monetize the site too early. I added some affiliate links, but they didn't convert well and I eventually removed them.

## Honest numbers.
Spent about 200 hours building and maintaining the tool and have acquired about 1,500 users so far. The site has generated $0 in revenue, but the positive feedback from users has been rewarding in and of itself. My cost is basically just my time and a small fee for hosting (about $5/month).

## Questions for the community
How do you balance the need to create something truly useful with the need to generate revenue? Also, are there any other solo builders out there who have successfully created free tools that generate income?

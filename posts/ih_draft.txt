# Building Tools for Foreigners in Japan: A Solo Journey
## The problem I saw
I've lived in Japan for a while now, and I've seen many foreigners struggle with the bureaucracy after arriving. From registering with the local authorities to setting up a bank account, it can be overwhelming. I wanted to create something that would make this process easier.

## What I built
I built a set of free tools, including an interactive checklist, to help foreigners navigate the bureaucracy in Japan. The checklist is filtered by visa type, so users can see exactly what they need to do. I've also created 12 other tools, from a Japanese address generator to a guide for finding apartments.

## What worked / What didnt
What worked was focusing on a specific pain point and creating a simple, easy-to-use solution. I also got lucky with some posts on subreddits like r/japanlife and r/movingtojapan, which drove some initial traffic to my site. What didn't work was trying to monetize the site too early - I added some affiliate links, but they didn't convert well, and I ended up removing them.

## The honest numbers
I've spent around 200 hours building and maintaining the tools, and I've had about 1,500 users so far. The site has generated $0 in revenue, but I've had some positive feedback from users, which is rewarding in itself. My costs are basically just my time, and some minor expenses for hosting (around $5/month).
